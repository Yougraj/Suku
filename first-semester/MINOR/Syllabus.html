🎯 MINOR Course: Philosophy of Education

🧠 Philosophy of Education Syllabus 🧠

Exploring philosophical foundations of education and their practical applications ✨

📋 Course Information

  • Course Title: Philosophy of Education
  • Course Code: MINOR
  • Nature of Course: MINOR (Discipline Specific Elective)
  • Total Credits: 4
  • Total Hours: 60 (52 Lecture + 8 Tutorial)
  • Distribution of Marks: 70 (End-Sem) + 30 (In-Sem) = 100

📘 Unit I – Introduction to Philosophy (12 Marks)

Marks Distribution: 12 | Hours: 13 Lecture + 2 Tutorial = 15 Total

🧠 1.1 Meaning, Nature and Scope of Philosophy

  • 📜 Definition and etymology of Philosophy
  • 📜 Nature and characteristics of philosophical inquiry
  • 📜 Scope and branches of Philosophy

🧠 1.2 Branches of Philosophy

  • 📜 Metaphysics: Nature of reality and existence
  • 📜 Epistemology: Theory of knowledge
  • 📜 Axiology: Theory of values (Ethics and Aesthetics)

🧠 1.3 Meaning, Nature and Scope of Education

  • 📜 Definition and concept of Education
  • 📜 Nature and characteristics of Education
  • 📜 Scope and dimensions of Education

🧠 1.4 Types of Education

  • 📜 Formal Education: Structured institutional learning
  • 📜 Informal Education: Lifelong learning process
  • 📜 Non-formal Education: Organized learning outside formal system

🧠 1.5 Relation between Education and Philosophy

  • 📜 Philosophical foundations of educational practice
  • 📜 How philosophy guides educational theory and practice

🧠 1.6 Meaning, Nature and Scope of Philosophy of Education

  • 📜 Definition and significance
  • 📜 Role in educational planning and implementation

🧠 1.7 Philosophy of Education and Educational Philosophy

  • 📜 Distinction between the two concepts
  • 📜 Practical implications for educators

📗 Unit II – Role of Philosophy in Education (12 Marks)

Marks Distribution: 12 | Hours: 9 Lecture + 2 Tutorial = 11 Total

🎯 2.1 Philosophical Foundations of Education and its Importance

  • 📜 How philosophical principles shape educational systems
  • 📜 Importance of philosophical grounding in education

🎯 2.2 Role of Philosophy in Education

  • 📜 Philosophy and Aims of Education: Goal setting and purpose
  • 📜 Philosophy and Curriculum: Content selection and organization
  • 📜 Philosophy and Methods of Teaching: Pedagogical approaches
  • 📜 Philosophy and Role of Teachers: Teacher's role and responsibilities
  • 📜 Philosophy and Discipline: Classroom management and student behavior

📙 Unit III – Indian Schools of Philosophy and Their Influences in Education (18 Marks)

Marks Distribution: 18 | Hours: 15 Lecture + 2 Tutorial = 17 Total

🕉️ 3.1 Basic Features and Classification of Indian Philosophy

  • 📜 Characteristics of Indian philosophical thought
  • 📜 Orthodox (Astika) and Heterodox (Nastika) schools

🕉️ 3.2 Yoga Philosophy

  • 📜 Concept of Yoga Philosophy: Patanjali's system
  • 📜 Hathayoga and Rajayoga: Different approaches
  • 📜 Astangika Yoga: Eight-fold path
  • 📜 Influence of Yoga Philosophy in Education: Holistic development

🕉️ 3.3 Vedanta Philosophy

  • 📜 Basic Tenets: Brahma, Atman, Jagat, Maya
  • 📜 Advaita Vedanta: Non-dualistic approach
  • 📜 Influence of Vedanta Philosophy in Education: Spiritual dimension

🕉️ 3.4 Buddhism

  • 📜 Concept of Buddhism: Buddha's teachings
  • 📜 Four Noble Truths of Buddha: Suffering and liberation
  • 📜 Middle Path: Balanced approach to life
  • 📜 Influence of Buddhism in Education: Compassion and wisdom

📕 Unit IV – Western Schools of Philosophy and Their Influences in Education (18 Marks)

Marks Distribution: 18 | Hours: 15 Lecture + 2 Tutorial = 17 Total

🏛️ 4.1 Basic Features of Western Philosophy

  • 📜 Characteristics and evolution of Western philosophical thought
  • 📜 Major periods and movements

🏛️ 4.2 Idealism

  • 📜 Basic Tenets: Reality as ideas and mind
  • 📜 Influence in Determining:
    • Aims of education
    • Curriculum design
    • Methods of teaching
    • Role of teacher and discipline

🏛️ 4.3 Naturalism

  • 📜 Basic Tenets: Nature as ultimate reality
  • 📜 Influence in Determining:
    • Aims of education
    • Curriculum design
    • Methods of teaching
    • Role of teacher and discipline

🏛️ 4.4 Pragmatism

  • 📜 Basic Tenets: Truth through practical consequences
  • 📜 Influence in Determining:
    • Aims of education
    • Curriculum design
    • Methods of teaching
    • Role of teacher and discipline
